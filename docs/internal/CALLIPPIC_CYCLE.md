## What is the Callippic Cycle?

The **Callippic cycle** is a calendar refinement period of **76 years**. It was proposed by the Greek astronomer Callippus around 330 BCE as an improvement on the Metonic cycle.

### Key Characteristics

- Length: 76 years (exactly 4 Metonic cycles of 19 years each, minus 1 day).
- It was designed to better align the lunar calendar with the solar (tropical) year over longer periods.
- While the Metonic cycle (19 years) has an error of about 1 day every 219 years, the Callippic cycle reduces this error significantly for its time.
- It contains 27,759 days and 940 synodic months.

Although superseded by more accurate systems (such as the Julian and Gregorian calendars), the Callippic cycle represents an early attempt to create longer-term calendar harmony between solar and lunar cycles.
