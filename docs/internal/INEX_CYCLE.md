## What is the Inex Cycle?

The **Inex cycle** is an eclipse periodicity of approximately **29 years minus 20 days** (more precisely 10,571.95 days or 358 synodic months). After one Inex period, a similar eclipse occurs, but with important differences from the Saros.

### Key Characteristics

- **Length**: ~29 years − 20 days.
- **Shift in season**: Each successive eclipse in an Inex series occurs about 1/3 of a year earlier in the calendar (roughly 4 months earlier).
- **Change in type**: Unlike the Saros, which tends to preserve eclipse type (total, annular, partial) for long stretches, the Inex more readily changes the character of the eclipse (e.g., total → annular or hybrid, or shifts in magnitude and duration).
- **Series duration**: A single Inex series can last 20,000–30,000+ years and contain hundreds of eclipses — far longer than a typical Saros series (1,200–1,500 years).
- **Number of active series**: There are many more Inex series active at any given time than Saros series because of the longer individual series length.

The Inex arises from a different near-commensurability of lunar months that realigns the Sun, Moon, and nodes, but with a phase relationship that produces the seasonal shift and more variable eclipse characteristics.

## Relationship to the Saros Cycle

The Saros and Inex are complementary:

- Many eclipses belong to **both** a Saros and an Inex series.
- The Saros preserves eclipse characteristics over long periods and shifts longitude by ~120° each cycle.
- The Inex shifts the season by ~4 months and tends to change eclipse type more readily.
- Together, they form a powerful predictive and analytical framework. The combination of Saros (stable, repeating character) and Inex (long-term evolution and seasonal drift) allows eclipse sequences to be tracked and understood over very long timescales.

In our model, the Saros can be viewed as providing relatively stable, repeating "notes" or perturbations, while the Inex provides a much slower "carrier wave" or evolutionary envelope that gradually transforms those notes over millennia.
