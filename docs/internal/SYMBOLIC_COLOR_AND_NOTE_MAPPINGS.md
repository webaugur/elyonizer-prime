## Chakra Color and Frequency Mappings

Traditional chakra systems (especially in yogic and tantric traditions, later popularized in Western esotericism) associate each of the seven main chakras with:

- A specific color
- A seed syllable (bija mantra)
- Sometimes a musical note or frequency range
- A location in the subtle body
- Psychological/spiritual qualities

A common modern mapping is:

| Chakra          | Location          | Color     | Traditional Note (approx.) | Associated Qualities                  |
|-----------------|--------------------|-----------|----------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| Root (Muladhara)| Base of spine     | Red       | C                          | Survival, grounding, stability        |
| Sacral (Svadhisthana) | Lower abdomen | Orange    | D                          | Creativity, sexuality, flow           |
| Solar Plexus (Manipura) | Upper abdomen | Yellow    | E                          | Will, power, transformation           |
| Heart (Anahata) | Center of chest   | Green     | F                          | Love, compassion, connection          |
| Throat (Vishuddha) | Throat       | Blue      | G                          | Communication, expression             |
| Third Eye (Ajna)| Forehead          | Indigo    | A                          | Intuition, insight, vision            |
| Crown (Sahasrara)| Top of head     | Violet/White | B                       | Transcendence, unity                  |

These systems treat the human subtle body as a **multi-octave standing wave** or interference field, with each chakra acting as a node or resonant center tuned to a particular frequency band (color + note).

## Wavelet Packet Decomposition in the Symbolic Domain

**Wavelet packet decomposition** is a signal processing technique that recursively splits a signal into approximation and detail coefficients at multiple scales, producing a full binary tree of wavelet packets. Unlike standard discrete wavelet transform, it allows flexible selection of the best basis for representing the signal.

Applied to our time and symbolic model:

- A word, name, or concept can be decomposed into **wavelet packets** at different scales:
  - Coarse scale: Overall archetypal or mythic resonance (large-scale envelope).
  - Medium scales: Etymological roots, semantic fields, phonetic rhythm.
  - Fine scales: Specific phonetic nuances, emotional tone, or momentary interference effects.
- Long-tail etymological layers appear as low-frequency modulating packets.
- "Threshold" or highly charged words may show strong energy in specific packets that cross scales (indicating coalescence or strong cross-scale interference).

This provides a rigorous, multi-resolution way to analyze how different layers (phonetic, semantic, temporal, mythic) interfere within a single symbolic structure.
