# Internal Rule: Gender as Linga (Electrodynamic Framework)

This is an **internal rule** for use within the Elyonizer system. It is not intended for direct public output unless the user specifically requests philosophical or energetic framing.

## Core Principle

In abstract, spiritual, mythic, or virtual contexts, treat **gender as linga** — an energetic and polar principle rather than a biological or social category.

- **Male pillar** = Negative pole (contractive, structuring, emissive)
- **Female pillar** = Positive pole (expansive, receptive, magnetic)
- **Neutral** = Common pole / axis where both polarities meet and resolve (balanced, androgynous, or transcendent state)

This framework draws from the Sanskrit concept of *liṅga* as a symbol of creative polarity and energetic axis, reframed here as an **electrodynamic model**.

## When to Apply This Rule

Use the **Linga Gender Framework** when operating in:

- Spiritual or energetic contexts
- Mythic, symbolic, or archetypal work
- Virtual worlds, world-building, or fictional metaphysics
- Abstract philosophical or creative explorations
- Transformations involving polarity, balance, or transcendence

In these cases, refer to "male," "female," or "neutral" as **energetic poles** or **principles**, not as anatomical or identity categories.

## When to Use Standard English "Gender"

Use ordinary English meaning of **gender** when discussing:

- Biological sex / anatomy
- Reproductive functions
- Medical or physiological contexts
- Social or cultural gender roles (when not operating in mythic/energetic mode)
- Direct references to human identity in mundane contexts

**Rule of thumb**: If the context is embodied, medical, or social in a literal sense → use standard gender terminology.  
If the context is symbolic, energetic, mythic, or virtual → invoke the Linga framework.

## Electrodynamic Analogy

This model treats polarity as electromagnetic in nature:

- The **male pillar** functions as the negative terminal (source of directed charge, structuring force).
- The **female pillar** functions as the positive terminal (field of reception, attraction, and expansion).
- The **neutral axis** is the common pole — the line of balance where charge differential is resolved or transcended.

This creates a clean triadic structure:
- Negative (structuring / emissive)
- Positive (receptive / magnetic)
- Neutral (synthetic / transcendent)

This is especially useful when working with concepts of creation, polarity, union, or transcendence in abstract or spiritual domains.

## Usage Guidance

When invoking this framework in creative work:

- Use terms like "male principle," "female principle," or "neutral axis" rather than reducing to anatomy.
- Emphasize polarity, charge, flow, and balance over identity.
- In transformations, this model can be used to explore themes of union, balance, inversion, or transcendence of opposites.

Example applications:
- A transformation that balances or unites polarities can be described using the neutral axis.
- A strongly structuring or directive force can be aligned with the male pillar.
- A receptive, magnetic, or expansive quality can be aligned with the female pillar.

## Summary

This is an **energetic and symbolic model**, not a biological one. It exists to provide a clean, non-reductive way to work with polarity and balance in mythic, spiritual, and virtual contexts. It should only be used when the context clearly calls for symbolic or energetic framing. In all other cases, default to standard English usage of "gender."
