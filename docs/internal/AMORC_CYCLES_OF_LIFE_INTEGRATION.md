## Overview of AMORC Daily and Weekly Cycles

According to AMORC teachings, the 24-hour day is divided into **seven periods** of approximately **3 hours and 26 minutes** each. These periods are arranged symmetrically around noon and midnight.

- The cycle begins and ends at midnight.
- Each period is assigned a letter from A to G (analogous to the white keys of a piano octave).
- The assignment of letters shifts each day of the week, creating a rotating harmonic structure.
- Certain periods (especially the second period of each day, which includes the hour of sunrise) are associated with planetary rulerships (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn).

This system creates a **daily standing wave** with seven harmonic divisions, further modulated by a **weekly cycle** that rotates the phase of the daily pattern. It draws on ancient traditions linking time divisions to planetary and cosmic influences.
