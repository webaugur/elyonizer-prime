# Elyonize Phoneme and Sound Mapping Reference

Use these mappings as a baseline for consistent, resonant adaptations. Prioritize euphony and natural phonetic flow (valid syllable structures, harmonious consonant combinations, and balanced vowel quality).

## Vowel Mappings (English source sounds)
- a (as in cat, about) → a or ā (prefer ā for emphasis or length)
- e (as in bed, see) → e, ai, or ī (contextual)
- i (as in bit, machine) → i or ī
- o (as in hot, go) → o, au, or ā
- u (as in put, rude) → u or ū
- y (as consonant) → y; as vowel often i or ī
- Long vowels: emphasize with macrons in transliteration and longer script forms (e.g., ā, ī, ū)

Diphthongs:
- ai, ay → ai or e
- au, aw, ow → au or o
- oi, oy → oi (rare; use ai + i or creative resolution)

## Consonant Mappings & Adjustments
Non-native or harsh sounds are adapted for euphony:
- f, ph → ph or v
- v, w → v or b (v is common; w often v or glide)
- z, s (voiced) → s, j, or retroflex sibilant
- sh, ch (as in ship) → ś or retroflex sibilant; prefer softer palatal quality
- th (voiceless) → th or retroflex equivalent
- th (voiced) → dh or retroflex equivalent
- ch (as in church) → c or ch
- j (as in judge) → j
- r (American rhotic) → r or vocalic r
- Clusters: simplify or use valid harmonious conjuncts where natural
- Aspirates: Add gentle aspiration where emphatic for elevated texture

## Common Structural Elements for Naturalization
- Strategic lengthening of vowels for gravitas
- Gentle compounding using resonant elements
- Preference for flowing endings that suggest place, quality, or abstract noun
- Avoidance of clusters that break euphony while preserving original rhythm

## Tips for Layered Construction
- Descriptive layering: adjective-like element + core
- Rhythmic lengthening before certain endings
- Creation of internal assonance or alliteration when it emerges naturally
- Keep total length reasonable; break very long words into logical, elegant segments

## When to Deviate
- If user requests "strict resonant" or "highest fidelity", stay very close to direct mapping.
- If "creative" or "elevated", lean into layered compounding and atmospheric coloring.
- Always offer at least resonant + enhanced options unless specified otherwise.
- For user-provided context (tone, use case, desired feeling), adjust depth accordingly.

This reference ensures consistency across sessions while allowing creative flexibility. It is a living document refined through practice.
